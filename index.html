Saturday

Pesce all'Acqua Pazza

Fish with White Wine & Cherry Tomatoes · 15 min prep · 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 2 fish fillets (cod, halibut, or snapper, ~6–8 oz each)
  • 1 pint cherry tomatoes, halved
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • 4 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • Small bunch fresh basil, torn
  • 1 lemon, for serving
  • Crusty bread, for serving

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium. Add garlic; cook 2 min until golden.
  2. Add cherry tomatoes; cook, pressing gently, until they burst, ~4 min. Season with salt.
  3. Pour in white wine; bring to a simmer and bubble 1 min.
  4. Nestle fish into the broth; spoon broth over top. Cover loosely and cook 5–7 min until fish flakes.
  5. Scatter torn basil over. Serve with lemon wedges and crusty bread.
Sunday

Sticky Miso Salmon Bowl

20 min prep · NYT Top 5 most-rated 2024 · 6 servings (dinner + 3 lunches)
Move chicken thighs from freezer to fridge tonight to thaw for Tuesday.

Ingredients

  • 1½ lbs skinless salmon fillet, cut into 6 portions
  • 3 tbsp white miso paste
  • 1½ tbsp soy sauce (low sodium)
  • 1½ tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp fresh ginger, finely grated
  • 1 tsp grapefruit zest (or lime zest)
  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 scallions, thinly sliced
  • 2 avocados, sliced (dinner only)
  • 3 nori sheets, cut into strips

Instructions

  1. Cook sushi rice per package. While hot, stir in butter and half the scallions.
  2. Whisk miso, soy sauce, honey, ginger, and grapefruit zest. Coat salmon all over.
  3. Broil on a foil-lined sheet pan 6–8 min until glaze is caramelized. Watch closely — honey burns.
  4. Divide rice into bowls. Top with salmon, avocado, nori, and remaining scallions.
  5. Lunches: store rice and salmon separately; add fresh avocado when serving.
Lunch tip: skip avocado in packed lunches — it won't keep. Add fresh when serving.
Monday · Vegetarian

Creamy, Spicy Tomato Beans & Greens

20 min prep · NYT #4 most-rated 2024 · 6 servings (dinner + 3 lunches)

Ingredients

  • 2 cans (15 oz) cannellini beans, drained (reserve liquid from 1 can)
  • ½ cup sun-dried tomatoes in oil, roughly chopped (+ 1 tbsp jar oil)
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes or tomato purée
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp crushed red pepper flakes
  • 4 oz baby arugula
  • 3 oz Parmesan, grated
  • ½ cup panko breadcrumbs
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Instructions

  1. Heat 1 tbsp sun-dried tomato oil in a large skillet over medium. Cook onion 5 min; add garlic and red pepper, 1 min more.
  2. Add sun-dried tomatoes, crushed tomatoes, beans, cream, and reserved bean liquid. Simmer 8–10 min until slightly thickened.
  3. Toast panko in 2 tbsp olive oil 3–4 min until golden; stir in half the Parmesan off heat.
  4. Stir arugula into beans and wilt 1 min. Top with remaining Parmesan and panko crumb.
Lunch tip: store without panko (goes soggy). Toast fresh panko at lunch — takes 3 min.
Tuesday

One-Pot Chicken & Rice with Caramelized Lemon

20 min prep + 35 min oven · NYT #1 most-rated 2024 · 7 servings (dinner + 4 lunches)
Preheat oven to 400°F before you start. The oven does the work — dinner is mostly hands-off.

Ingredients

  • 2½ lbs bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 3 lemons (2 sliced into rounds, 1 for wedges)
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 3½ cups chicken broth
  • ¾ cup kalamata olives, pitted
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Crushed red pepper flakes
  • Fresh parsley, for serving

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Pat chicken dry; season with salt, pepper, and oregano.
  2. Brown chicken skin-side down in 1 tbsp butter over medium-high, 5–6 min. Transfer to a plate.
  3. Cook lemon slices in drippings, 2–3 min per side, until caramelized. Transfer.
  4. Add remaining butter and garlic; cook 30 sec. Add rice; stir to coat. Pour in broth and olives; bring to a simmer.
  5. Nestle chicken skin-side up on rice. Lay caramelized lemons over top. Roast 30–35 min until rice is cooked and chicken reaches 165°F.
  6. Scatter parsley over. Serve with lemon wedges.
Tuesday night: move shrimp from freezer to fridge to thaw for Wednesday.
Wednesday

Lemony Shrimp and Bean Stew

25 min prep · NYT all-time top 50 · 7–8 servings (dinner + 3 lunches)
Shrimp should be fully thawed (moved from freezer Tuesday night). Pick up crusty bread this morning.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp marinade:
  • 2 lbs large shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • 2 tsp lemon zest · 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika · salt & pepper
  • Stew:
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 large onions, sliced into half-moons
  • 4 garlic cloves, thinly sliced
  • ¾ cup dry white wine
  • 5 cups chicken broth
  • 4 cans (15 oz) cannellini beans, undrained
  • 3 tbsp lemon juice · 2 tsp lemon zest
  • 4 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Fresh parsley & chives

Instructions

  1. Toss shrimp with lemon zest, minced garlic, paprika, salt, and pepper. Set aside.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium. Cook onions 6–8 min until soft and golden. Add sliced garlic, 1 min more.
  3. Pour in wine; reduce by half, ~2 min.
  4. Add broth and undrained beans. Simmer 8 min until slightly thickened.
  5. Add shrimp; simmer gently 3–4 min until just pink and cooked through.
  6. Stir in lemon juice, lemon zest, and butter until melted. Top with parsley and chives. Serve with crusty bread.

Prep Guide

~25 minutes of Sunday prep sets up the whole week.

Saturday · When You Get Home
  • Put chicken thighs and shrimp in the freezer immediately
Sunday Prep (~25 min)
  • Mince all garlic for Mon–Wed (~16 cloves). Store in a small jar in the fridge.
  • Dice 1 onion for Monday; slice 2 onions into half-moons for Wednesday. Store separately.
  • Basil: trim stems, store in a glass of water on the counter. Parsley & chives: wrap in damp paper towels, fridge.
  • Move chicken from freezer to fridge to thaw for Tuesday.
Monday (~20 min)
  • Sauté onion, add beans and tomatoes, stir in cream, wilt arugula
  • Toast panko fresh (3 min) — do not store with dish
  • Serve with bread
Tuesday (~20 min prep + 35 min oven)
  • Preheat oven to 400°F before starting
  • Brown chicken, caramelize lemon, add rice and broth, into the oven
  • Use oven time to relax — mostly hands-off
  • Tonight: move shrimp from freezer to fridge
Wednesday (~25 min)
  • Marinate shrimp (5 min), soften onions (8 min)
  • Add wine and broth, simmer beans (8 min), cook shrimp (4 min)
  • Pick up crusty bread this morning
